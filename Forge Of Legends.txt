SKILLTREE


Every skill falls into one of these categories:


* Stun skills (prevents players from moving for a turn)


* Continuous damage skills (debuffs: Burn, bleeds, life drain, etc) 


* Continuous empowerment skills (buffs that grow, etcetera) 


* Heavy damage skills (Pretty straightforward. They deal heavy damage, and usually non magically. Basically, strikes that require melee) 


* Sacrificial skills (Sacrifice something to gain something. For example, sacrificing a percentage of health to get a huge buff. Since you're sacrificing, the buffs are usually very large) 


* Summoning skills (Summons helpers to deal damage)


* Cleansing skills (Skills that cleanse debuffs) 


* Rejuvenation skills (Skills that rejuvenate health)

BATTLE SYSTEM 




There are three different battle modes in play:


-Farming in Monster Zones
-Boss Raids
-PVP




Farming
Farming entails traveling to monster zones and fighting varied difficulty monsters to gather EXP. It is one of the most consistent ways of leveling up. Monsters killed will drop rewards according to their strength. If a player is stronger than the enemy, melee damage is often enough to vanquish them.


There is also an option for “Auto Battle”, where you attack a large group of monsters nearby and fight them all. Battle switches to automatic mode, and it automatically shuffles between your equipped skills. All you have to do is monitor your health and mana, to prevent death. 




Boss Raids
This type of battle entails multiple players fighting against a single boss. These are usually found in raids or missions. Players create/join a party and take turns dealing damage to the boss monster. The leader of the party takes damage first, followed by whoever is next IF/WHEN the leader is vanquished. 




PVP
Player VS Player mode. This mode allows two players to compete with each other in tournaments, with decided resources/equipment on the line. It could also be a casual sparring session between two players. 




Battle Format
Which brings us to the battle format. During exploration, after detecting a monster, a panel like this will appear:


[You have encountered Class-E monsters: Groundhog Turtles]


[Fight]    [Run]    [Auto-battle] 


… 
[You have encountered a Class-A monster: Abyssal Presbyter] 


[Fight]   [Run] 


…
After accepting a battle, it will have a panel like this:


[Fighting Monsters…..]


[Health]   [Mana] 


…


[Melee]   [Skill]


[Health]   [Mana] 




Melee is basically physical blows with your weapons. Your base 100% attack. 


If you click skill, your list of available skills will pop up, and you can choose from them. Some skills are non-magical. The skills that require magic will consume mana. Once your mana is depleted, you can't use magic skills.




FOOTNOTE


* It is very much possible to die while farming, especially in “Auto Battle” mode. If you die while farming/exploring, you can only resurrect after a full day, and with half of your items gone. As such, it is paramount that you take note of your HP and Mana, and use potions to refill from time to time. 
* Dying in PVP or Boss Raids won't incur any penalty. However, it is impossible to use potions or eat food when in battle. You can only prepare in advance. 
* Using the “run” option during explorations deducts coins from your inventory.

PROFESSIONS






Players are allowed to pick any one of these professions:




* Miner: A profession that entails exploring caves and underground networks to excavate precious ores and runestones. 


* Forager: A profession that entails forest exploration to acquire all sorts of fruits.


* Angler: A profession that involves fishing. 


* Herbalist: A profession that entails gathering special roots, branches and herbs. 


* Hunter: A profession that entails hunting animals for their meat and bones. 




The following professions are handled by NPCs. 


* Blacksmiths: A profession that converts all sorts of ores into gear. 


* Alchemists: A profession that converts special roots, branches, bones, and herbs into potions. 


* Cooks: A profession that entails using berries, fruits, fish and meat to cook foods with varying effects. 


* Enchanters: A profession that entails improving the state of equipment using runestones. 




Profession EXP Bar 
Every profession comes with an exp bar, and filling them up will result in a level-advancement. The exp bar is cleared after each level growth, and you have to fill it up once more. Below are the points needed for each level:




* Level 0-1 = 0/100 points (can find only one resource at a time) 
* Level 1-2 = 0/200 points (can find only one resource at a time) 
* Level 2-3 = 0/400 points (30% chance of finding two resources at a time) 
* Level 3-4 = 0/800 points (50% chance of finding two resources at a time) 
* Level 4-5 = 0/1600 points (70% chance of finding two resources at a time) 
* Level 5-6 = 0/3200 points (100% chance of finding two resources at a time) 
* Level 6-7 = 0/6400 points (12% chance of finding three resources at a time) 
* Level 7-8 = 0/12,800 points (24% chance of finding three resources at a time) 
* Level 8-9 = 0/25,600 points (32% chance of finding three resources at a time) 
* Level 9-10 = 0/51,200 points (50% chance of finding three resources at a time)

RANKS/ADVANCEMENT 




PLAYERS RANKS


Each Rank contains 100 Levels within. 


* Wanderer: A soul merely stepping into the world. (0-100)


* Follower: A soul who admires a certain path. (101-200)


* Disciple: Now walks a certain path. (201-300)


* Master: Is now a master of that path. (301-400)


* Exalted: Praised as a lord of his path. (401-500)


* Crown: A leader. (501-600)


* Supreme: An absolute monarch. (601-700)


* Legend: A being spoken of in legends. (701-800)


* Myth: A true fable. (801-900)


* Eternal: An everlasting being. (901-1000)




As such, a player may be referred to as “A Level 302 Master”, “A Level 50 Wanderer”.




PLAYER ADVANCEMENT


Advancement occurs in two ways:


-Level Ups
-Rank Ascension


Level Ups
In order to level up, one simply needs to partake in battles and gather EXP. The most consistent way of gathering experience points is through Farming. Raid bosses in events give higher EXP, but only occur from time to time. 


* A growth multiplier is used to multiply the current EXP bar for every level increased. For example, 100 EXP is needed to go from level 1 to 2. Upon reaching level 2, we use a multiplier on the 100 EXP. Upon reaching level 3, we use a multiplier on level 2’s EXP bar.


Rank multipliers = ×1.10


For example:


1 — 2 = 100 EXP
2 — 3 = 110 EXP
3 — 4 = 121 EXP
4 — 5 = 133 EXP (etc… all the way to 100)


EXP bar resets upon reaching a new rank, with a solid increase in its base amount.

* Base Stat Attributes = 10; Welcome Points Bonus =20

Rank Ascension 
Rank Ascensions are more difficult, as it comes with higher bonuses. In order to move from one rank to another, players will need to gather ingredients which correspond to whatever Deity they worship. 


Ingredients:
* Heart of the Red Phoenix, Gem of Luminance. (God of Flames) 


* The Void-Eye, Orb of Silence. (God of Darkness) 


* Crown of Fortune, Tears of The Endless Goldfish. (God of Abundance) 


* Core of a Fallen Star, Fruit of World Tree. (Goddess of Stars) 


* Divine Heart Essence, Forgotten Desire Seed. (Goddess of Love) 


* Scales of Equilibrium, Adonai Sword. (God of Justice) 


* Ink of Time, Eye of All-knowing. (God of Knowledge) 






FOOTNOTE


* Ingredients cannot be gathered or farmed like any other resource. Rather, they are specifically bestowed after certain achievements: Increasing Faith level, participating in major Events, etc. 
* The number of ingredients needed multiplies by 2 with each rank. (i.e If a Wanderer needs 1x Void-Eye, 1x Orb of Silence to advance to Rank 2, it becomes 2x each the next time, and 4x each the next time.. Etc.)

Each monster grade affects what they can drop:

* C = (100% chance for 80 - 120 Coins, 100% chance for any common resource, 50% chance for any uncommon resource, 10% chance for any rare resource, 10% chance for any standard potion, 5% chance for C-grade Runestone)

* S = (100% chance for 900 - 1200 Coins, 50% chance for any legendary resource, 10% chance for any mythic resource, 30% chance for any greater potion, 5% chance for an S-grade Runestone)

POTIONS 




There are generally two categories of Potions sold by NPCs:


* Exploration-related potions. 
* System-related potions. 




EXPLORATION




Health Potions (Affects health replenishment) 


* Minor HP Potion → +20% HP instantly (40-60 Coins) 


* Standard HP Potion → +40% (100-150 Coins) 


* Greater HP Potion → +70% (250-400 Coins) 




Mana Potions (Affects Mana replenishment) 


* Minor Mana Potion → +20% Mana instantly (40-60 Coins) 


* Standard Mana Potion → +40% (100-150 Coins)


* Greater Mana Potion → +70% (250-400 Coins)




Luck Potion (Affects everything related to gathering resources, monster drop rate) 


* Minor Luck Potion: +5% Luck (40-60 Coins) 


* Standard Luck Potion: +15% Luck (100-150 Coins) 


* Greater Luck Potion: +30% Luck (250-400 Coins) 




Insight Potion (Affects EXP gain.)


* Minor EXP Potion: +5% EXP gain (40-60 Coins) 


* Standard EXP Potion: +15% EXP gain (100-150 Coins) 


* Greater EXP Potion: +20 EXP gain (250-400 Coins)








SYSTEM 




* Class Reset Potion: Allows players to reset class, and thus, skilltree. Keeps stat points that have been gathered prior. (Cost = 5000 Coins) 




* Race Rebirth Potion: Allows players to change their race. (Cost = 10,000 Coins) 




* Divine Shift Potion: Allows players to change their Deity. However, this results in a reset of “Faith level” too. (Cost = 15,000 Coins) 




* Stat Reset Potion: Allows players to reset their stat points, for new redistribution across all attributes. (Cost = 2000 Coins) 




* Companion Change Potion: Allows players to select a new companion. This resets the companion’s level. (Cost = 6000 Coins) 






FOOTNOTE 


* Only HP and Mana potions are stackable and can be used indefinitely during exploration. 
* EXP and Luck potions can only be used once a day, and they last 3 hours.

Sudden monster attack. 








TITLE SYSTEM 


Players earn titles based on actions that affect a large number of NPCs or a wide region of the map. Titles give status in the roleplay aspect of the game. For example:


* Savior of Whitecrest Village 


* Slayer of Profanities








NPC RELATIONSHIP SYSTEM


NPCs remember players. Build your bond with them by helping out or conversing. 


Levels:


* Hostile


* Neutral


* Friendly


* Trusted


* Revered




Most NPCs generally start as being neutral with players. Benefits of increasing bond with NPC:  Discounts, special quests, Gifts, Special dialogue. 


Robbing, or not helping an NPC will result in your bond with them dropping to hostility. It may also result in them filing a complaint with the authorities, and you getting arrested. Getting arrested make you lose a portion of your inventory. 








MORAL / CHOICE EVENTS


Let players decide:


“Stranger is being attacked!”


* Help victim → gain bond


* Ignore → nothing


* Join bandit → gain gold but lose bond








PROFESSION RANDOM EVENTS

“Bloom Surge” → double resources
